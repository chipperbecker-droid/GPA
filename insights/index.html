Featured perspective

To sell, or not to sell?

An owner’s answer should reflect personal readiness, company readiness, market reality, and a credible plan for what follows—not a reaction to a single difficult season.

Every business will eventually change ownership or cease to exist. Thoughtful owners prepare for that reality before circumstances make the decision for them.

For many entrepreneurs, the company is both their largest asset and a defining life experience. A sale should therefore begin with honest questions: Is the desire to exit durable? Is the company performing well? Is management depth sufficient? What characteristics of the business will matter to qualified buyers?

Building services transactions are influenced by market area, service sectors, labor model, customer mix, contract quality, and other operating factors. Owners cannot change every characteristic, but they can understand how those characteristics affect likely outcomes and improve what is within their control.

A well-prepared owner also considers life after the transition. The sale of a company can mark the beginning of a new chapter, but only when the personal plan receives the same thought as the transaction plan.

Modernized summary of Gary Penrod’s legacy article, “To Sell, Or Not Sell; That Is The Question.”
