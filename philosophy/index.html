Our philosophy

Add value. Protect trust. Leave the business stronger.

Since GPA’s founding, the central idea has remained simple: advisory work should create a result that serves the real needs of the people involved and still makes sense when viewed in retrospect.

The standard

Good outcomes require more than closing.

A successful project is one that is understood, aligned, thoughtfully executed, and durable, not merely one that reaches a signature page.

GPA dedicates its industry knowledge and energy to owners who may divest, buyers who may invest, and leaders who need experienced assistance with strategic or operational questions.

Each assignment is approached as a distinct situation. Market conditions matter, but so do the owner’s priorities, the buyer’s capabilities, the company’s people, and the realities of transition. GPA’s role is to help clients understand those forces and act with clear eyes.

How we work

Principles that shape the process.

These are not slogans. They are practical disciplines for protecting clients and improving decisions.

01

Complete confidentiality

Private information is shared selectively and only within a disciplined process. Prospective counterparties are expected to document their commitment to confidentiality.

02

Candid, practical judgment

Clients deserve a realistic view of value, readiness, timing, risk, and buyer fit, even when the most useful advice is not the easiest advice to hear.

03

Alignment before momentum

The interests and expectations of the parties should be understood early. Speed is useful only when it moves the right transaction in the right direction.

04

Industry knowledge in context

Experience helps GPA recognize patterns, but no company is reduced to a template. Every assignment is evaluated on its own operating and human facts.

05

Respect for the human side

A transaction can affect identity, family, employees, customers, and communities. Those realities belong in the planning, not outside it.

06

Professional coordination

M&A requires legal, tax, accounting, financial, and operational expertise. GPA respects those roles and helps keep the wider advisory team connected to the business objectives.

Adding value in practice

Experience should change the quality of the decision.

GPA’s original philosophy begins with a commitment to dedicate industry knowledge and energy to people who want to divest, invest, or address an important business project.

Adding value means more than introducing a buyer to a seller or producing an analysis. It means helping the client define the objective, gather and understand relevant information, recognize tradeoffs, prepare for difficult questions, and lead the work toward a conclusion that serves the stated need.

Diligence and thoroughness are central because a transaction is judged not only on closing day. GPA believes it should still appear cost-effective and successful when the parties look back after the transition has taken place. That standard encourages realistic expectations and discourages decisions driven only by short-term pressure.

A mature advisory team also matters. GPA associates have brought experience as former company owners, building services managers, financial officers, and M&A professionals. Depending on the assignment, the firm can draw on that collective operating and financial perspective rather than treating the engagement as a one-person exercise.

Fit, value, and execution

Every company and every transaction is different.

GPA takes a flexible approach because two companies with similar revenue may differ significantly in customer mix, management, geography, labor model, contract quality, and owner dependence.

In intermediary work, considerable care should be given to matching a prospective buyer with the seller. Financial capacity is only one part of the match. Strategic fit, operating ability, culture, transition expectations, reputation, and the intended treatment of customers and employees can all influence whether a transaction is likely to endure.

The same realism applies to market valuation. GPA’s approach is intended to be thoughtful and prudent, not designed simply to confirm an owner’s preferred number or a buyer’s preferred discount. A useful valuation recognizes both the company’s strengths and the characteristics that a qualified market participant may regard as risk.

Timing matters, but urgency should not create unnecessary disruption. GPA seeks to move a project in the optimum amount of time while limiting avoidable interference with the client’s operations, workforce, and customer relationships. Preparation and controlled communication are the tools that make that possible.

The human side and confidentiality

There are lives behind the assets and numbers.

An owner’s company may represent a lifetime of work. Employees may depend on it for their livelihood, customers may depend on continuity of service, and a buyer may be committing substantial capital and personal responsibility.

Change can be difficult even when the economics are sound. GPA helps clients think through communication, leadership continuity, workforce concerns, customer relationships, the seller’s post-closing role, and the personal transition that follows ownership. These issues are not peripheral; they can influence whether the business performs as expected after closing.

Confidentiality protects that process. GPA limits the release of private corporate information and expects prospective counterparties to document their commitment to confidentiality before receiving sensitive material. Information is shared in stages, based on relevance and qualification, rather than distributed broadly.

Business acumen alone is not enough. Professionalism requires care in communication, respect for every party’s role, coordination with legal and financial advisors, and the discipline to protect the client even when doing so slows the process.

“Our goal is to add value to the transaction or project through diligence, thoroughness, and experienced advisory leadership.”
