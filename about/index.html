Gary A. Penrod

Founder & Managing Associate

Gary founded and operated a successful building services company for 15 years before selling the business to an international service group.

Following the sale, he assisted that organization with additional acquisitions. The experience gave him perspective on both sides of a transaction: the owner’s personal and operational concerns, and the acquirer’s need for sound information, disciplined evaluation, and a workable transition.

In 1988, Gary founded Gary Penrod & Associates, Inc. as an industry-specific consulting firm. GPA evolved to specialize in M&A intermediary and business advisory services for firms engaged in building services.

Industry leadership and credentials

Knowledge built through participation, not observation.

Gary’s understanding of the building services industry began with the demands of operating a company and continued through a career devoted to transactions, valuations, and owner advisory work.

After the sale of his contracting company, Gary assisted the acquiring international service group with other acquisitions. That work deepened his understanding of how buyers assess companies in different markets and how transaction decisions appear from the acquirer’s side of the table.

He founded GPA in 1988 as an industry-specific consulting firm. It quickly evolved toward intermediary services for people selling or buying building services companies, together with valuation-related and strategic advisory work for industry owners. The focus remained narrow by design: Gary believed that an advisor could contribute more when the firm understood the operating environment, the buyer community, and the people involved.

Gary has written for Services and Contracting Profits, spoken to industry audiences internationally, and made presentations through Building Service Contractors Association International and Fulcrum Information Services. He served BSCAI for years as a board member and later as its president.

His valuation and transaction perspective has been applied full time since the mid-1980s. GPA’s standard is to provide a prudent, realistic view of market value—one that can support sound decisions by owners, buyers, lenders, and investors rather than simply produce an attractive number.

A long view of value

The most useful assessment is one that remains understandable when the transaction is reviewed years later. That principle connects Gary’s valuation work to GPA’s broader philosophy of durable outcomes.
