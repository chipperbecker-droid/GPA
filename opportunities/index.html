Buyer interest in the legacy market

GPA’s network included strategic and investor buyers.

The original opportunities page explained that GPA regularly received inquiries from qualified prospective buyers seeking building services companies of different sizes.

Those inquiries included both strategic acquirers already operating in the industry and investors interested in entering or expanding within the sector. The legacy page specifically identified interest in the Midwest, Southeast, and Mid-Atlantic.

GPA asked prospective buyers to describe their preferred company profile, including revenue size, market sector, and geographic preference. Owners considering a sale were encouraged to begin learning about preparation and process before deciding whether to enter the market.

That disciplined approach remains useful: a buyer should be clear about criteria and capacity, while an owner should understand the process, information requirements, likely buyer groups, and confidentiality safeguards before sensitive details are released.

For prospective buyers

A more useful inquiry starts with clear criteria.

When contacting GPA, briefly describe your acquisition experience, preferred geography, target revenue or size, service mix, funding readiness, and timing. Please do not send sensitive personal or financial documents by ordinary email.

What may be requested

  • A signed confidentiality agreement
  • Information about acquisition criteria and strategic fit
  • Evidence of financial capacity or funding plan
  • Respect for the seller’s communication process
