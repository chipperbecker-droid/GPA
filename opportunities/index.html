Northeast janitorial services provider

Founded in 1998 · Northeast United States

Business overview

The company was an established janitorial services provider serving supermarkets, retail stores, banks, and large pharmacies. It supported large customers across the Northeast as well as accounts in other states. Services included routine cleaning, disinfection, and floor care.

Investment highlights

  • Reputable customers in desirable market sectors
  • Recurring contract revenue and long-term national-client relationships
  • Growth potential through expansion with large customers and broader use of operations in other states
  • An experienced management team

Operating model

The company operated through a subcontract model using established janitorial subcontractors vetted through an approval process. Management’s supervision and processes were described as supporting account profitability and quality control.

Financial context

The legacy listing expected 2020 adjusted EBITDA of approximately $3 million and noted that new 2021 accounts were not included in the annualized first-quarter revenue figure. It also clearly disclosed that the financial statements were still being completed.

Financial-record limitations

The company had historically used cash-basis accounting, with information distributed across company and customer billing systems. Expenses associated with other real-estate businesses had also been paid through the janitorial company and needed to be isolated. Management hired financial support and a new controller to consolidate records and complete 2020 and year-to-date 2021 reporting before GPA prepared a confidential information memorandum.

Ownership and transition

The two owners had managed the company since its 1998 founding. One owner expressed a desire to remain after a transaction, while the other was willing to stay if requested by the buyer.

Historical process: Detailed information was to be provided to qualified parties through a confidential information memorandum after execution of a mutual nondisclosure agreement.

Buyer interest in the legacy market

GPA’s network included strategic and investor buyers.

The original opportunities page explained that GPA regularly received inquiries from qualified prospective buyers seeking building services companies of different sizes.

Those inquiries included both strategic acquirers already operating in the industry and investors interested in entering or expanding within the sector. The legacy page specifically identified interest in the Midwest, Southeast, and Mid-Atlantic.

GPA asked prospective buyers to describe their preferred company profile, including revenue size, market sector, and geographic preference. Owners considering a sale were encouraged to begin learning about preparation and process before deciding whether to enter the market.

That disciplined approach remains useful: a buyer should be clear about criteria and capacity, while an owner should understand the process, information requirements, likely buyer groups, and confidentiality safeguards before sensitive details are released.

For prospective buyers

A more useful inquiry starts with clear criteria.

When contacting GPA, briefly describe your acquisition experience, preferred geography, target revenue or size, service mix, funding readiness, and timing. Please do not send sensitive personal or financial documents by ordinary email.

What may be requested

  • A signed confidentiality agreement
  • Information about acquisition criteria and strategic fit
  • Evidence of financial capacity or funding plan
  • Respect for the seller’s communication process
