Advisory services

Practical guidance for the full life of a business.

GPA supports building service contractors before, during, and after pivotal decisions—from improving readiness to navigating a transaction with confidence.

Transaction advisory

Clarity at every stage.

The work begins with the owner’s or buyer’s objectives, not a predetermined process. GPA helps define the right path, surface risks early, and keep momentum without losing judgment.

Sell-side advisory

For owners exploring or preparing for a sale. GPA helps evaluate readiness, frame the company’s market position, prepare information, identify qualified buyers, manage communications, support negotiations, and coordinate the transaction through diligence and closing.

Buy-side advisory

For strategic, financial, and first-time buyers. GPA can help define acquisition criteria, assess opportunities, understand industry-specific operating factors, support due diligence, and bring practical context to structure and transition planning.

Transaction support

GPA works alongside a client’s legal, accounting, financial, and operational advisors. The role is to help keep business issues visible, decisions informed, communication disciplined, and the transaction aligned with the client’s goals.

Industry advisory

Building service companies have distinct drivers: recurring contracts, labor, customer retention, service mix, geography, and management depth. GPA brings these realities into the conversation from the beginning.

Planning & positioning

Prepare the business, not just the deal.

A better transaction often starts well before a buyer enters the picture. GPA helps owners understand what can be strengthened now and what tradeoffs deserve attention.

Valuation & market positioning

From informal market assessments to more detailed valuation-related work, GPA helps owners understand the factors that influence value and marketability. The appropriate scope is defined before work begins; tax and legal conclusions remain with the client’s qualified professionals.

Exit planning

Clarify timing, personal goals, transition expectations, likely buyer profiles, and the operating improvements that can support a more resilient company and a more credible future process.

Strategic consulting

Objective guidance on long-term planning, business restructuring, operational alignment, financial needs, management resources, and other projects where an experienced industry perspective can add value.

Succession & transition

Consider the human side of change: leadership continuity, workforce stability, customer relationships, ownership transition, and the owner’s own plan for the period after a transaction.

A measured process

What working together looks like.

Each engagement is scoped around the client’s circumstances. A typical first phase focuses on objectives, timing, business context, and whether GPA is the right fit.

01 / Understand

Start with context

Discuss the business, the people involved, the decision at hand, and what a successful outcome would mean.

02 / Assess

Define the real questions

Identify readiness, information needs, value drivers, likely obstacles, and the right sequence of work.

03 / Act

Move with discipline

Build the plan, manage communication, coordinate advisors, and keep decisions tied to the client’s priorities.
